Hello, Imperfection!

March 18, 2023

I often find myself afraid to do anything for fear of being imperfect. Both in my professional life as a software engineer and in my personal life, I have this desire to be perfect even though I know perfection is the enemy of continuous improvement. Being perfect means not making mistakes. It means accomplishing something, looking back, and saying, "There's no way I could have done that any better." I don't want that for myself. That sounds like a lot like complacency. I want to challenge myself, make mistakes, and learn from them.

So here's to imperfection, to putting my desire to continuously improve into action, and to putting myself out there when it would have been a whole lot easier if I just did nothing.

Until next time,
Dayton Bobbitt
